             <h4 style="font-family:KaiTi;font-size:30px; margin:10px;color:#d84178;">介绍</h4>
       <p style="font-size:21px;font-family:KaiTi;margin:10px;">王周淑涵博士是新加坡教育部母语和华文课程的海外顾问。她同时是ELE国际咨询公司总裁，也是亚洲协会中文儿童及沉浸式项目大联网主任。她曾任美国马里兰大学国家外语中心的副主任、亚协中文项目执行长，以及特拉华州教育厅外语和国际教育督学。除了学术著作，她还编写了两套幼儿园到十二年级（K-12）中文教育系列教科书。王博士毕业于宾夕法尼亚大学，专修教育语言学。 </p><br/>
       <h4 style="font-family:KaiTi;font-size:30px; margin:10px;color:#d84178;"> 简介</h4>
          <h4 style="font-family:KaiTi;font-size:30px; margin:10px;color:#d84178;"> 精心浇灌母语园地: 一些思考及策略</h4>
       <p style="font-size:21px;font-family:KaiTi;margin:10px;">
       新加坡2020年人口普查数据显示，在华族家庭中，英语的使用频率已经超过母语。这个趋势顺应了语言和文化的变迁特质，即语言和文化是流动且具渗透性的，也会随着各种因素的改变而持续演变。在新加坡这样一个多语社会中，已经形成英语、华语、马来语及淡米尔语四种官方语言并存的语言体系，而英语和母语在交际沟通、传承文化等方面扮演的角色不尽相同。本讲座带着听众深入了解为何说语言是个人、族群及国家的人力、文化和社会资产，并思考如何在新加坡维护和加强母语的问题。讲员提出在每个家庭中精心浇灌母语小园地的概念，并为家庭和学校如何落实这个目标提供10个策略。在结语部分，讲员会鼓励听众并分享有关资源。</p>
<br/>
<h4 style="margin:10px;color:#d84178;">Profile</h4>
   <p style="margin:10px;">
Dr Shuhan C. Wang is a consultant for Chinese Language curriculum projects for the Ministry of Education, Singapore. She is presently the President of ELE Consulting International and Director of the Chinese Early Language and Immersion Network, Asia Society. She was Deputy Director of the National Foreign Language Center, University of Maryland, Executive Director for Chinese Language Initiatives at the Asia Society, and Supervisor for World Languages and International Education for the State of Delaware, U.S.A. She has published numerous papers, book chapters, and two series of Chinese Language textbooks for K-12. She earned her Ph.D. from the University of Pennsylvania.</p><br/>
 
<h4 style="margin:10px;color:#d84178;">Synopsis</h4>
       <h4 style="margin:10px;color:#d84178;">Cultivating the Gardens of Mother Tongue: Considerations and Strategies</h4>    
<p style="margin:10px;">
An infographic of the 2020 Singapore census data shows that there are now more ethnic Chinese homes speaking English as the most frequently spoken language, as compared to Mother Tongue Languages (MTL). This is a natural trend, in a multilingual context, as languages and cultures readily transform and shift because they are by nature porous, in flux, and constantly evolving when exposed to new stimuli. Being a multilingual society, Singapore has developed its varieties of English and MTLs. The discussion leads the audience to deepen their understanding of how languages are human, cultural, and social capital for individuals, groups, and society at large; and to rethink the question of how to maintain MTLs in the Singapore context. The presenter proposes the idea of growing MTL gardens in every home and offers 10 strategies that families and schools can adopt to achieve this goal. The presenter concludes with words of encouragement and shared resources.
